Anger Management:

Anger management is when a person has anger management issues, it usually means that they have trouble managing their anger and often act out their anger in a destructive, rather than constructive, manner. It is normal for humans to sometimes lose control of their tempers by means of yelling or screaming.

The goal of anger management is to reduce both your emotional feelings and the physiological arousal that anger causes. You can't get rid of, or avoid, the things or the people that enrage you, nor can you change them, but you can learn to control your reactions.

Anger can be caused by both external and internal events. You could be angry at a specific person (such as a coworker or supervisor) or event (a traffic jam, a canceled flight), or your anger could be caused by worrying or brooding about your personal problems. Memories of traumatic or enraging events can also trigger angry feelings.

Anger is one of the many emotions that humans experience. Anger is not “good” or “bad”. It can be helpful when it motivates you to take positive action, but it can also feel uncomfortable and drive behaviours that can cause problems in day-to-day life.

Anger management refers to recognizing and managing anger in healthy ways. It's helpful for avoiding confrontations and maintaining relationships. Here are a few key strategies:

1. Recognize Triggers: Identify which causes you angry. Understanding these triggers will help you better control your reactions.

2. Use Relaxation Techniques: Deep breathing, progressive muscle relaxation, or mindfulness can help quiet your mind and body when anger rises.

3. Include "I" statements: Express your feelings without blaming others. For example, instead of expressing "You never listen," say "I get frustrated when I don't feel heard."

4. Take a Timeout: Walk away from the situation to calm down. A little break can help you recover perspective.
